Allison Morgan

I’m a UX & Human Factors researcher based in Oakland, CA. I design and run end-to-end human-subjects studies—across driving simulators, in-vehicle setups, and remote sessions—using multimodal instrumentation (eye-tracking, physiological sensors, VR/AR) to turn complex signals into clear, decision-ready insights for AI-supported HMIs.

Most recently, I was a UX Researcher on the Human Interactive Driving team at Toyota Research Institute, where I led multimodal studies, built repeatable QA/ops pipelines, and helped design systems that improve driver performance and safety.

I earned my B.S. in Cognitive & Behavioral Neuroscience from UC San Diego. I’m excited about cross-device experiences, repairable/meaningful hardware, and GenAI features that are safe, inclusive, and genuinely useful.
